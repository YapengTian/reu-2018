Instructions (Please read if this is your first time) (Click to expand)

TL;DR: We are artificial intelligence researchers looking to expand our dataset. Given a label of some sounds, we want to annotate when, in a 10-second video, you see the object actually producing those sounds. Sometimes the video will not show the object or the object may not make the sounds, so you should handle that video accordingly.

If you are using Internet Explorer 8 or below, please do not accept these HITs as we cannot guarantee they'll work.

STEPS TO SUCCESSFULLY COMPLETE THESE HITS:

  1. Look at the label, and description of the label, that describes the sounds you will hear in this video.
  2. Click the button and watch the video.
    • The YouTube video player will then play the specified 10-second clip for you. The 'Play Video' button will change to a button. The clip will play on repeat until you click the 'Pause Video' button.
    • Controls in the YouTube player are disabled. We disabled the controls so we can show you one specific part of the video.
    • Please ignore any advertisements that may play during the YouTube video. Use of an ad-blocker is highly suggested (for speed, so you don't have to wait for ads to play) but not required.
  3. Determine which category your video falls into and follow the corresponding instructions (note, all categories are of equal importance and take the same amount of time to complete):
    • If you SEE the object that makes the sounds described by the label AND that object produces the described sounds AT THE SAME TIME:
      • Mark the SEE AND HEAR radio button.
      • Use the annotator under the video to mark when (accurate to the nearest 1-second) this occurs in the video.
      • Note: Still images CANNOT fall in this category.
      • Purpose: Videos in this category will go into our dataset. We want these videos.
      • Example: The label says 'Water'. For the whole video you hear splashing and rushing of water. Only during the 4th-7th seconds you actually see the water. You would select the 'SEE AND HEAR' option, and annotate the 4th-7th box in the annotator.
    • If you HEAR the object that makes the sounds described by the label BUT that object NEVER appears in the video:
      • Mark the HEAR radio button.
      • Use the annotator under the video to mark when (accurate to the nearest 1-second) you hear the object.
      • Purpose: This category of videos will help us understand how the dataset we are pulling from was constructed. Finding these videos will verify the integrity of the dataset.
      • Example: The label says 'Fireworks'. The video is of the night sky, and at the last second you hear a boom. The video ends before you see any light, however. You would select the 'HEAR' option, and solely annotate the last box in the annotator.
    • If you SEE the object that makes the sounds described by the label BUT that object NEVER produces the described sounds:
      • Mark the SEE radio button.
      • Use the annotator under the video to mark when (accurate to the nearest 1-second) you see the object.
      • Purpose: This category of videos will show us potential flaws in the dataset we are pulling from. These videos will help improve how researchers collect large quantities of data.
      • Example: The label says 'Tool'. The video shows a power-sander for the whole 10-second clip. The power-sander (a tool) is never turned (so no sound is produced). You would select the 'SEE' option, and annotate every box in the annotator.
    • In the rare case where you SEE AND HEAR the object, but NEVER at the same time:
      • Decide which, SEE or HEAR, has more time in the video, and mark its corresponding radio button. In the case of a tie, choose SEE.
      • Use the annotator under the video to mark when (accurate to the nearest 1-second) you either see or hear the object.
      • Purpose: See the categories above.
      • Example: The label says 'Racecar'. The video starts black but you hear racecars for 3 seconds. The last 7 seconds of the video shows racecars driving but you never hear them. You would select the 'SEE' option (7 seconds > 3 seconds), and annotate the last 7 boxes in the annotator.
    • If the video is UNRELATED to the label:
      • Mark the UNRELATED radio button.
      • Provide us, in the textbox, an explanation for why the label is unrelated to the video.
      • Purpose: These videos will help us improve the dataset we are pulling from. The makers of the dataset we are pulling from want to know the bad videos.
      • Example: The label says 'Snoring'. The whole video is of a coffee-maker brewing a cup of coffee. Obviously the label is unrelated to the video, and you are confused because this rarely happens. You would select the 'UNRELATED' option, and explain in the textbox that the video showed a coffee-maker instead of a person snoring.
  4. Click submit! Thank you for completing our HIT!

Note: If this is your first time doing these HITs, please tell us, in the first comments box, your favorite flavor of ice cream just so we know you read the instructions :) (you should only do this on your first time!).

HOW TO USE THE ANNOTATOR:

Each box under the video indicates a 1 second segment. If the category you chose is present in a segment, click the box for that segment. To highlight a box, you would left click the box. To un-highlight a highlighted box, you would left click the box again. You can click and drag to highlight (and un-highlight) multiple boxes. Under each box is a button that seeks to that segment of the clip. When the video is playing, you should see a bright yellow in the box that the video is currently on. Here are the colors with their respective meaning:

Not Selected Selected Not Selected Selected
Video Not Playing Here Video Not Playing Here Video Playing Here Video Playing Here
  • Example: if the label is 'Oink' and you see a pig in the clip and the pig oinks during the fourth and fifth second, you would highlight the fourth and fifth box.
  • Example: if the label is 'Speech' and you see a person speaking during the whole clip, you would highlight every box.
